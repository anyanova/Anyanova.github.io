Who I am

Nothing is more complicated than the environment. Studying ecology taught me that the right questions are usually simple ones, even when the data is messy and tangled.

Aldo Leopold famously said that "one of the penalties of an ecological education is that one lives alone in a world of wounds." I have found that it is also to live in a world of wonder. I live in Flagstaff, AZ and enjoy my weekends playing in rivers, hiking, climbing, and exploring the natural beauty of home.

I'm an extrovert who relishes a hard problem and a good team. I pride myself in my ability to turns data into actionable narratives and find solutions to hard problems. I value flexibility, creativity, new challenges, and am looking for the next step in my career.
