Who I am

My job title said ecologist. The actual work was data, people, and translation, and that part travels anywhere.

For twelve years at the U.S. Geological Survey I built a program from nothing: designed the data collection, wrote the analysis, trained the hundreds of people who fed it, then told the story well enough that it reached the AP and National Geographic.

I like hard problems, big spreadsheets, and the moment someone finally gets it. Trilingual, happy on a stage or in a terminal, and looking for the next thing worth explaining.

What I've done

Selected work

  • 2024–2026

    Ran communications for a federal science campus

    Social Media Strategist / Public Affairs · USGS

    Built and published content for a national audience, organized a multi-day workshop on big data and AI applications at Lowell Observatory, and created an art-meets-science expo that pulled a demoralized campus back together. Also translated dense IT service updates into bulletins people actually read.

  • 2012–2024

    Built a 200-person volunteer data program from scratch

    Program design, training, QA · Seven states

    Designed the protocols, recruited and trained the network, handled the quality control, and turned the output into peer-reviewed results. Wrote the invited primer on how to do this well, and co-authored the paper defining the field's terminology.

  • 2012–2026

    Analysis lead on multi-scale datasets

    Ecologist · USGS Grand Canyon Monitoring & Research Center

    Bayesian regression, mixed effects models, and spatial statistics in R across genetic, acoustic, and field datasets spanning a seven-state watershed. Presented findings directly to the managers making decisions on them.

  • 2024

    Quality assurance on $12M federal grant reviews

    Federal Reviewer · Dept. of Commerce, NTIA

    Reviewed competitive broadband grant applications and budgets ranging from $5M to $12M, checking other reviewers' work for consistency and defensibility.

  • 2025–2026

    Consolidated decades of scattered records into a working model

    Research Analyst · University of Montana

    Pulled multi-decadal seed and planting data out of four organizations' incompatible systems, built one clean record, and modeled outcomes in R and GIS. Paper accepted at Frontiers in Ecology and the Environment.

  • 2016–2024

    Made technical research land in national media

    Science communication

    Research I led or co-authored was covered by the Associated Press (picked up by the New York Times and Washington Post), National Geographic, Scientific American, NPR, High Country News, and Arizona PBS. Guest on five podcasts and an invited speaker from Hong Kong to Brisbane.
